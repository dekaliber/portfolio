“No matter where you go, there you are”

-Confucius

Prior to college, I introduced myself using my full Chinese name, Tairan, which means “calm and composed” in Mandarin. A friend from freshman year started calling me Tai, and it has since had the profound effect of transforming my name from something that is butchered on a weekly basis to one that is just occasionally misspelled.

I grew up in China and, like our pilgrim fathers, settled in New England. I spent my formative years there and ultimately graduated from Cornell University with an AB in Information Science. Afterwards, I spent seven years in the wintry suburbs of Chicago, where I learned to hate snow. I currently live in sunny Los Angeles with my wife.

“When I let go of what I am, I become what I might be”

-Lao Tzu

Like most college grads who didn’t want to be poor, I took a lucrative corporate job at an industrial supply company in a rotational management track. In this former life, I’ve managed and delivered several successful software projects, served as art director for a nearly 4,000 page catalog, managed a warehouse operation responsible for over 10,000 orders a day, and headed a finance department that deals with many, many more zeros. It was a stable and comfortable living, and I learned many aspects of how to run an incredibly successful business.

However, aside from rare moments, work was rarely a source of passion. It was the definition of “working to live,” and I found it increasingly difficult to justify spending half of my day doing something I wasn’t enthusiastic about in return for a steady uptick in my savings account. It was time for a change, and in the beginning of 2014, I finally stepped off of the hamster wheel.

I am currently enjoying a sabbatical and taking the opportunity to learn voraciously, explore creative endeavors, get back in shape, read, travel, and generally reset. However, I’m open to the right opportunity should it come along, and I would be particularly excited about any roles in product management or design for a company doing interesting, ambitious work. If you feel like I’d be a good fit for a role you have in mind, I’d love to hear from you.

“You can’t depend on your eyes when your imagination is out of focus”

-Mark Twain

Since I was young I have always loved the process of creating. I have been building websites since 2001 (though, if I’m honest, the first few years probably weren’t very good). In my youth, I would invent board games and create levels for video games. Before I got involved with computer graphics, I spent a lot of time drawing despite the fact that my parents regarded it as a poor way to advance their ambitions for me to become a doctor or lawyer.

I try often to indulge in my other semi-serious pursuits, which are photography, music, food, and motorsports. I also love a great film, and while I am especially receptive to post-apocalyptic/dystopian sci-fi or historical epics featuring ample hand-to-hand combat, I am generally happy to watch anything with a decent score on Rotten Tomatoes.
